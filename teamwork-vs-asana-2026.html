    3. Head-to-Head Feature Comparison

    Time Tracking & Billing Teamwork Wins

    Teamwork: Includes native time tracking across all paid plans. You can set different billing rates for different staff members, mark time as billable or non-billable, and export invoices directly.

    Asana: As of 2026, Asana still lacks a robust, native billing and time-tracking engine. You'll need to integrate with Harvest or Toggl, which adds cost and data silos.

    Winner: Teamwork. For an agency, this is often a dealbreaker.

    Client Management Teamwork Wins

    Teamwork: Offers a dedicated Client Portal. You can invite unlimited clients for free, allowing them to see progress, approve files, and add tasks without seeing your internal team's private chatter.

    Asana: You can add guests, but the permissions are less "agency-friendly." It's harder to hide internal-only information while giving clients a professional experience.

    Winner: Teamwork. It's built for the "client-facing" reality.

    Task Management & UI/UX Asana Wins

    Teamwork: Functional, but let's be honest‚Äîit feels like 2018. The interface is a bit dense and utilitarian.

    Asana: Beautiful, modern, and clean. The "Multi-home" task feature (putting one task in multiple projects) is a lifesaver for cross-functional teams.

    Winner: Asana. No one beats Asana in terms of "joy of use."

    Project Views & Automation Asana Wins

    Teamwork: Offers the basics (List, Board, Gantt). Automation is helpful but can be rigid.

    Asana: Offers List, Board, Timeline, Calendar, Portfolio, and Workload views. Their "Rules" engine is incredibly powerful, with over 200 triggers to automate almost any workflow.

    Winner: Asana. It is significantly more flexible and powerful for complex process automation.

    Profitability Tracking Teamwork Wins

    Teamwork: Features a native "Profitability Report" that compares your project budget (in dollars or hours) against the actual cost of your team's time. This shows you which clients are profitable and which are draining resources.

    Asana: None. You would need to export data to a tool like PowerBI or Google Sheets to get these insights.

    Winner: Teamwork. This is a unique, high-value feature for agency owners.

    10. FAQ

    Q1: Which is better: Teamwork or Asana?

    For agencies that bill clients hourly or on retainer: Teamwork. For general teams with internal projects: Asana. The answer depends entirely on your business model.

    Q2: Is Teamwork cheaper than Asana?

    Yes, significantly for agencies. Teamwork Deliver ($9.99/user) includes time tracking and billing. Asana Starter ($10.99/user) + Harvest ($10.80/user) = $21.79/user. Teamwork saves 54% for agency use cases.

    Q3: Does Asana have time tracking?

    No native time tracking. Asana relies on third-party integrations like Harvest, Toggl, or Everhour. This adds cost ($10-12/user/month) and creates data silos.

    Q4: Which is better for agencies?

    Teamwork. It was built specifically for the agency business model with native time tracking, client billing, profitability reports, and client portal features that Asana doesn't have.

    Q5: Can Teamwork replace Asana?

    Yes, functionally. However, your team might complain about the UI for the first few weeks. If you need Teamwork's billing features, the trade-off is worth it. If you don't need billing, Asana's superior UI makes it the better choice.

    Q6: Which has better automation?

    Asana. Their "Rules" engine is more advanced and flexible with 200+ triggers. Teamwork has basic automation but it's less powerful for complex multi-step workflows.

    Q7: Is Teamwork good for non-agencies?

    It works, but it feels "over-engineered" if you don't need the billing features. For internal teams without client billing needs, Asana, ClickUp, or Monday.com are better fits.

    Q8: Which has a better free plan?

    Asana (10 users vs. Teamwork's 5 users). Asana's free plan is also more generous with features, making it better for very small teams testing PM tools.

    Q9: Does Teamwork integrate with Asana?

    Not natively. You could use Zapier to connect them, but there's rarely a reason to use both tools simultaneously. Choose one based on your billing needs.

    Q10: Which is easier to learn?

    Asana. It's much more intuitive for the average user with a cleaner interface and simpler feature set. Teamwork has a moderate learning curve due to its depth of agency-specific features.

    Q11: What about client billing features?

    Teamwork has native invoicing integration, billing rates per team member, and profitability tracking. Asana has none of this - you'd need to export data to accounting software manually or pay for additional tools.
