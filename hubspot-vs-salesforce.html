3. The "Hidden" Costs of Implementation

Small businesses often look only at the license cost. But in 2026, the real cost is "Implementation and Maintenance."

A Salesforce implementation for 15 users typically costs between $10,000 and $25,000 in consultant fees. HubSpot can often be set up by a tech-savvy manager in a weekend, though their "Onboarding Fee" for Professional tiers is mandatory (usually around $3,000).

4. Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Which CRM is better for a team of under 10 people?

HubSpot is the clear winner here. Their Free and Starter tiers are specifically designed for small teams to scale without needing an IT department.

Is Salesforce too big for small businesses?

Not necessarily. Their "Starter Suite" is a great entry point, but as you grow, the complexity increases significantly compared to HubSpot.

Can I use my own domain with these CRMs?

Yes, both platforms allow for custom domains for your landing pages and support portals on their paid plans.

Does HubSpot have AI features?

Yes, HubSpot's "Breeze AI" is integrated across the entire platform, helping you write emails, summarize calls, and predict lead quality.

Is it hard to migrate from Salesforce to HubSpot?

No, there are specialized tools and partners that make this transition smooth. HubSpot even has a native Salesforce integration to help with the data move.

5. Final Verdict

For most small businesses reading SaaSRadar, HubSpot is the winner. It offers the best balance of power, ease of use, and marketing integration. However, if your business requires extreme customization and you have the budget for a consultant, Salesforce remains an elite choice.
