    2. What is Copper CRM?

    Copper isn't just a CRM that "connects" to Google; it is built on Google.

    Company Background

    Originally launched as ProsperWorks, the company rebranded to Copper to reflect its focus on "relationship management" rather than just "sales tracking." Being Google Ventures-backed gave them early, deep access to Google's APIs, allowing them to build a level of integration that competitors like HubSpot or Pipedrive simply cannot match legally or technically.

    The 2026 Market Position

    In a 2026 market flooded with AI-powered "all-in-one" platforms, Copper has doubled down on its niche: Simplicity. While Salesforce tries to be everything to everyone, Copper lives inside your Gmail sidebar. You don't "log in" to Copper as a separate task; you just open your email.

    Why It's Different

    • No Separate Login: For most daily tasks, you stay inside Gmail
    • Relationship-Focused: It's designed to track the quality of a long-term client relationship, not just the "close" of a transactional sale
    • Automatic Data Capture: It scrapes email signatures and calendar invites to build contact profiles so you don't have to

    3. Key Features (2026)

    Gmail & Calendar Integration

    The 2026 version of the Gmail sidebar is more powerful than ever. When you open an email, the sidebar immediately shows the contact's history, open deals, and recent files. You can add a new lead or update a deal stage with one click without leaving the thread. The Google Calendar integration is equally seamless‚Äîit automatically logs meetings as "activities" in the CRM, meaning you never have to manually prove you had a discovery call.

    Google Drive & Docs Integration

    Copper automatically links files attached to emails to the relevant deal record. In 2026, it also features "Shared Doc Intelligence," which surfaces Google Docs or Slides that have been shared with a prospect but not yet attached to the CRM record, suggesting you link them for team visibility.

    AI Features 2026: "Copper Intelligence"

    Relationship Intelligence

    The system automatically populates contact details by scraping email signatures. If a contact changes their phone number or job title in their signature, Copper flags it and asks if you want to update the record.

    11. Gmail Integration Deep Dive

    How the Gmail Sidebar Works

    The Copper sidebar appears on the right side of Gmail whenever you open an email. It instantly displays:

    • Contact's full profile and company information
    • All open deals and opportunities
    • Recent email conversations and meeting history
    • Attached files from Google Drive
    • Quick actions: Create deal, add task, log note

    Automatic Contact Creation

    When you receive an email from a new contact, Copper automatically creates a contact record by parsing the email signature. It extracts name, company, phone number, and title without any manual input.

    One-Click Deal Creation

    From any email thread, you can create a deal with one click. Copper automatically associates all emails in that thread with the deal, creating a complete communication history.

    Mobile Gmail Integration

    The Gmail integration works on mobile devices too. When you open the Gmail app on iOS or Android with Copper installed, you get the same sidebar functionality (adapted for mobile screens).

    14. Migration Guide

    From Spreadsheets or Gmail Contacts

    Time: Almost instant. Copper automatically imports your existing Google Contacts. For spreadsheets, export to CSV and import (takes 5-10 minutes).

    From Salesforce or HubSpot

    Time: 1-2 weeks for full migration including workflows.

    Process: Export CSV from old CRM, import to Copper. Contact and deal data transfers smoothly. Expect to rebuild custom workflows and automations.

    Challenge: Email history won't transfer. Copper will start logging emails from the migration date forward, but can retroactively scan your Gmail for past conversations with imported contacts.

    15. FAQ

    Q1: Is Copper worth it if I use Google Workspace?

    Yes. It's the only CRM that respects your time by not making you leave your inbox. For Google Workspace users, the seamless integration justifies the premium price.

    Q2: Can I use Copper without Google Workspace?

    No. Copper requires a Google Workspace (or Gmail) account to function. Without Gmail, you lose 90% of Copper's value.

    Q3: Does Copper work with Outlook or Microsoft 365?

    No. There is no Outlook integration. If you use Microsoft 365, consider HubSpot or Microsoft Dynamics instead.

    Q4: Is there a free trial?

    Yes, Copper offers a 14-day free trial with full access to all Professional tier features. No credit card required to start.

    Q5: Can I migrate from Salesforce or HubSpot to Copper?

    Yes, via CSV export/import. Most contact and deal data transfers successfully. Expect to rebuild custom workflows (1-2 weeks). Copper offers migration support on Business and Enterprise plans.

    Q6: What happens to my data if I cancel?

    You can export all your data as CSV files before canceling. Copper provides 30 days after cancellation to download your complete data export.

    Q7: Is Copper good for high-volume sales teams?

    Not really. Copper excels at relationship management, not transactional high-volume sales. For 100+ calls/day teams, use Close CRM instead.

    Q8: How does Copper compare to Pipedrive for Gmail users?

    Copper has far superior Gmail integration (native vs extension). Pipedrive is cheaper and has better visual pipeline management. Choose Copper if Gmail integration is your top priority. See our CRM comparison.

    Q9: Does Copper have built-in calling?

    No. You need to integrate with third-party services like RingCentral or Aircall. Unlike Close CRM which has native calling, Copper requires add-ons for phone functionality.

    Q10: Can I use Copper for marketing automation?

    Limited. Copper has basic email sequences but is not a marketing automation platform. For advanced marketing, you'll need to integrate with Mailchimp, HubSpot, or similar tools.
