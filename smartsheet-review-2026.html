The Core: Grid View & Cell Linking

The "Grid" is Smartsheet's heart. It feels exactly like Excel, but with "magic" columns for checkboxes, dropdowns, and contact lists.

Cell Linking: Unlike Excel, where linking workbooks often leads to broken links, Smartsheet allows you to pull a single cell's data into a master sheet with 100% reliability. This is the "secret sauce" for portfolio reporting.

Hierarchy: You can indent rows to create subtasks, which automatically roll up data (like dates or % complete) to the parent row.

Multiple Views: More Than a List

While the Grid is home base, you can toggle any sheet into:

  • Gantt Chart: Built-in dependencies and critical path highlighting
  • Card/Kanban: For those who want a Trello-style experience
  • Timeline View: A newer 2026 addition for high-level roadmap planning
  • Calendar: Syncs with Outlook/Google to see deadlines visually

The Automation Engine (No-Code Power)

Smartsheet's automation is arguably the most robust in the space. You can build "If This, Then That" workflows in seconds:

  • "If a task is marked 'At Risk,' send a Slack alert to the Manager and a formal email to the VP"
  • "When a row is added via a Form, auto-assign it to a technician based on the 'Region' column"

Forms: The Intake Hero

Smartsheet Forms are the gold standard for request management. They support conditional logic (e.g., if a user selects "Hardware," show the "Laptop Model" field). Submissions populate a sheet instantly, triggering your automations.

ü§ñ AI Features 2026: "Smartsheet AI"

In 2026, Smartsheet has integrated AI that actually helps rather than distracts:

  • Formula Assistant: Type "Calculate the average margin for all rows where status is 'Complete'" and the AI writes the complex formula for you
  • Smart Summaries: Get an AI-generated paragraph summarizing the health of a project based on 500 rows of data
  • Auto-Categorization: AI can read unstructured text in a cell and tag it by sentiment or department

11. FAQ

Q1: Is Smartsheet better than Excel?

For data analysis alone? No. For managing people, dates, processes, and collaboration? Absolutely yes. Smartsheet is Excel's evolution for teams.

Q2: How does Smartsheet compare to Monday.com?

Monday.com wins on visual appeal and ease of use. Smartsheet wins on data power, automation complexity, and enterprise features. Choose Monday if your team values aesthetics; choose Smartsheet if they value Excel-like data control.

Q3: Is Smartsheet worth the price?

At $19/user/month (Business plan), it's mid-range pricing. Worth it if you're consolidating Excel chaos, need enterprise security, or manage complex operations. Not worth it if you just need simple task lists.

Q4: What companies use Smartsheet?

Over 90% of Fortune 100 companies including Cisco, Netflix, Hilton, and Duke Energy. Popular in construction, manufacturing, finance, and operations-heavy industries.

Q5: Is Smartsheet good for construction?

Excellent for construction. The Gantt charts with dependencies, forms for field reporting, and ability to handle 20,000+ rows make it ideal for complex construction projects with multiple subcontractors.

Q6: Does Smartsheet replace Microsoft Project?

For most teams, yes. Smartsheet offers similar Gantt capabilities with better collaboration and modern cloud features. Microsoft Project is more powerful for ultra-complex projects but has a steeper learning curve.

Q7: Is Smartsheet easy to learn?

If you know Excel, you'll be productive in 1-2 days. If you've never used spreadsheets, expect 1-2 weeks. Much easier than learning Airtable or complex tools like Salesforce.

Q8: Does Smartsheet have a free plan?

Technically yes (1 user, 2 sheets), but it's basically useless for real work. Think of it as a trial rather than a functional free plan. For teams, expect to pay $9-19/user/month.

Q9: Can Smartsheet handle large projects?

Absolutely. 20,000 rows per sheet, unlimited sheets per workspace, portfolio-level reporting across hundreds of projects. This is why Fortune 100 companies use it.

Q10: What is the best alternative to Smartsheet?

If you need similar spreadsheet power but prefer databases: Airtable. If you want visual boards: Monday.com. If you need simpler PM: Asana. If you're enterprise: Microsoft Project or Wrike.

Q11: Is Smartsheet good for small businesses?

Only if you have 10+ employees and Excel-heavy workflows. Under 10 people, the $19/user cost adds up quickly. Consider cheaper alternatives like Asana or Basecamp for small teams.
